üìö Technical Analysis Guide

üìà MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)

MACD is a momentum indicator that shows the relationship between two moving averages of a security's price.

Buy Signal: MACD line crosses above signal line with positive histogram
Sell Signal: MACD line crosses below signal line with negative histogram

How it works: When the faster EMA (12-period) crosses above the slower EMA (26-period), it indicates upward momentum. The signal line (9-period EMA of MACD) helps confirm the trend.

‚ö° RSI (Relative Strength Index)

RSI measures the speed and magnitude of price changes, oscillating between 0 and 100 to identify overbought and oversold conditions.

Buy Signal: RSI below 30 (oversold condition)
Sell Signal: RSI above 70 (overbought condition)

How it works: RSI compares the magnitude of recent gains to recent losses over a 14-period timeframe. Values below 30 suggest the stock may be oversold and due for a bounce, while values above 70 suggest it may be overbought.

üìä SMA (Simple Moving Averages)

Simple Moving Averages smooth out price data to identify trends by calculating the average price over a specific number of periods.

Buy Signal: Price above SMA20 and SMA20 > SMA50
Sell Signal: Price below SMA20 and SMA20 < SMA50

How it works: When shorter-term averages (SMA20) cross above longer-term averages (SMA50), it indicates an upward trend. When the current price is above both moving averages, it suggests strong bullish momentum.
